VAR Match Workflow

A seamless process from incident to decision, ensuring fairness and transparency.

1

Incident Occurs

A potential VAR incident happens during the match.

2

Detection by VAR

VAR scans the game feed and detects a possible clear and obvious error.

3

Inform Referee

VAR informs the on-field referee using the encrypted headset.

4

Review Initiated

Referee decides to review (On-Field Review) or relies on VAR advice.

5

Video Review

VAR replays the incident from multiple angles in the control room.

6

Analysis & Rec.

VAR analyses the footage and gives a recommendation to the referee.

7

Referee Decision

Referee makes the final decision based on review and VAR input.

8

Communicated

Final decision is communicated clearly to players, coaches and spectators.

IFAB Compliant Interventions

OptiEYE VAR Lite strictly adheres to international protocols, intervening only for critical match-changing situations.

Goal / No Goal

To check if the goal was scored fairly, including offside and ball-out-of-play checks.

Penalty / No Penalty

To verify penalty area incidents, fouls, or handball infractions leading to a spot kick.

Direct Red Card

To review serious foul play, violent conduct, or denial of obvious goal-scoring opportunities.

Mistaken Identity

To ensure the correct player is penalized and sanctioned with a card by the referee.
